2026, the job market is... horrid, to say the least. Massive layoffs, human workforce replaced by AI that... doesn't do thing better that much, if at all.
This brings us to two perspectives. Mine, and my mother's.

For someone who found have just found something he loves and feels belong to, such realities are often disappointing, and devastating. But being a lover, I must find a way. If not directly, then indirectly; that's fine by me, the waiting game is nothing to someone determined.

But for a mother, seeing her son walking into what looks like a job version of a death trap, strong reactions and emotions are expected. But a mother must recognize her son's ambitions when it shows. For my case, it can't be more evident. That aside, she is so insistent on her views that it is harmful for both of us; not to mention the fact that it is flawed and certain aspects are amiss. She is a busy office worker, only seeing me so many times in the week. Yet she uses such limited info to make assumptions about my current state of being, some of which are... just wrong, hurting even.
