# Dear code,
2026, the programming job market is... horrid, to say the least. Massive layoffs, human workforce replaced by AI that... doesn't do that much better, if at all.
This brings us to two perspectives. Mine, and my mother's.

#### Me
For someone who found have just found something he loves and feels belong to, such realities are often disappointing, and devastating. But being a devotee, I must find a way. If not directly, then indirectly; the long game will not shake me so long as I maintain my poise.

#### Mother
For a mother, seeing her son walking into what looks like a job version of a death trap, strong reactions and emotions are expected. But a she, too, must recognize a son's ambitions when it shows. For my case, it can't be more evident. That aside, she is so insistent on her views that it is harmful for both of us; not to mention the fact that it is flawed and missing in certain aspects. She is a busy office worker, only seeing me so many times in the week. Yet she uses such limited info to make assumptions about my current state of being, some of which are... just wrong, hurting even.

#### Coda
My time is different from hers, drastically so. And it is about time she acknowledged it. The work culture has also changed as the newer generations slowly realize the harsh reality of jobs and competitions - not much different from a young soldier who was once eager to serve realize the horrors of the war he fights in.
But at the same time, I have to stay vigilant and adaptive if I want to go through with my future plans - as is the boy in war - even if it means stepping away from certain routes.
